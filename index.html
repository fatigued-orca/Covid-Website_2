Covid has impacted our lives as well as the lives of many others. We created this to help spread the message of that impact.

Introduction

The first case of Covid 19 was reported on December 31st, 2019 in Wuhan, China. Since then, the virus has travelled almost globally restricting interaction between people. Nobody expected our lives to be impacted as heavily as it has. The lifestyle we used to live has changed drastically and we want to speak about it. This website offers some of the latest news on Covid-19 and other helpful resources like physical and mental remedies to keep you safe and sane during quarantine.

This graph represents 5 major cities, in the state of California, harboring corona virus cases. These cities include San Diego, San Francisco, Los Angeles, Sacramento, and San Jose. Personal Experiences

Personal experiences of the students

Tyler Hadsabout

Quarantine affected my school life in a negative way at first because I didn't have any self discipline to do my work. When I found that discipline, it became easier to do school online. My health and fitness also deteriorated for the first few months. I didn't work out for a long time and when I played pickup for the first time in 4 months, the basketball was like a bowling ball. But I started working out with my family 3 days a week and we stay on it. I still miss going out and hanging with my friends but I have to be safe not to get my family sick.

Jeter Garcia

Quarantine affected my school life and took a toll on my mentality because of the isolation away from my friends. My family and I are always cautious in public areas when there are multiple people around. Although, it is one of the reasons of why I started to get into coding and programming, isolation and staying home forced me into using my computer more often, revealing my passion for code.

Angus

Quaratine has affected my mental and physical health, it has isolated me from a lot of people. Being unable to do sports and go to clubs has really changed my final year in highschool. It is very hard to focus in school and keeping up with my daily life being at home day. I'm very paranoid being around large groups of people. I'm also afraid to be alone in San Francsico due to the increase in Asian hate crimes.

William Nguyen

Once quarantine begun, things were starting to be more relaxing, but I was anxious in how I would be able to learn in school. It affected my life physically and mentally. Physically, I did not exercise enough and my muscle mass begun to deteriate. At first I thought I was losing weight, I was happy about that, but it was in a negative way. Mentally, the way I acted in front of my family and friends, it was more irrational and sporatic. My family wasn't really affected by the disease, but we were affected non-directly.
